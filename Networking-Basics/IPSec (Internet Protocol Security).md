## What is IPSec?  
- **Definition:** A suite of protocols used to secure IP communications by authenticating and encrypting each IP packet in a communication session.  
- **Purpose:** Ensures secure data transmission over untrusted networks like the internet → widely used in VPNs.  
- **Analogy:** Like sealing a letter in a tamper-proof envelope before sending it.  

---

## The 4 Core Steps of IPSec Operation  

### 1. Security Association Setup (Step 1)  
- **Function:** Establishes parameters for secure communication.  
- **Role:** Defines encryption algorithms, keys, and policies.  
- **Examples:** IKE (Internet Key Exchange) negotiation.  

---

### 2. Authentication (Step 2)  
- **Function:** Verifies identity of communicating devices.  
- **Role:** Ensures only trusted parties exchange data.  
- **Examples:** Pre-shared keys, digital certificates.  

---

### 3. Encryption (Step 3)  
- **Function:** Data is encrypted before transmission.  
- **Role:** Protects confidentiality of packets.  
- **Examples:** AES encryption.  

---

### 4. Secure Transmission (Step 4)  
- **Function:** Encrypted packets are sent across network.  
- **Role:** Ensures integrity and confidentiality during transit.  
- **Examples:** Site-to-site VPN communication.  

---

## IPSec Protocols  
- **AH (Authentication Header):** Provides integrity and authentication.  
- **ESP (Encapsulating Security Payload):** Provides encryption and integrity.  

---

## Modes of IPSec  
- **Transport Mode:** Encrypts only payload.  
- **Tunnel Mode:** Encrypts entire IP packet.  

---

## Why It Matters  
- **Network Security:** Protects sensitive data over internet.  
- **VPN Backbone:** Core technology for secure tunnels.  
- **Data Integrity:** Prevents tampering and spoofing.  

---

## Quick Recap (Mnemonic)  
- **Setup → Authenticate → Encrypt → Transmit**  
  - **SA → Identity → Protection → Secure Flow**
