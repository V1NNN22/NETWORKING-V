## What are IDS and IPS?  
- **Definition:** Security systems used to detect and prevent malicious activities in a network.  
- **Purpose:** Protect networks by identifying threats (IDS) and actively blocking them (IPS).  
- **Analogy:** IDS is like a CCTV camera (detects), IPS is like a security guard (detects + stops).  

---

## The 4 Key Differences Between IDS and IPS  

### 1. Functionality  
- **IDS Function:** Monitors and detects suspicious activity.  
- **Role:** Alerts administrators but does not block traffic.  
- **Examples:** Intrusion alerts in logs.  

- **IPS Function:** Monitors and actively blocks threats.  
- **Role:** Prevents malicious traffic in real-time.  
- **Examples:** Dropping malicious packets.  

---

### 2. Placement  
- **IDS Function:** Placed out-of-band (passive monitoring).  
- **Role:** Does not interfere with traffic flow.  

- **IPS Function:** Placed inline (active monitoring).  
- **Role:** Directly controls traffic.  

---

### 3. Response  
- **IDS Function:** Generates alerts and logs.  
- **Role:** Requires manual intervention.  

- **IPS Function:** Automatically blocks or mitigates threats.  
- **Role:** Immediate response.  

---

### 4. Impact on Network  
- **IDS Function:** No impact on traffic performance.  
- **Role:** Passive system.  

- **IPS Function:** May introduce slight latency.  
- **Role:** Active filtering system.  

---

## Key Comparison  

| Feature | IDS | IPS |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Action | Detect only | Detect + Prevent |
| Mode | Passive | Active |
| Placement | Out-of-band | Inline |
| Response | Alert | Block |

---

## Why It Matters  
- **Security:** Protects against cyber attacks.  
- **Monitoring:** Provides visibility into threats.  
- **Prevention:** Stops attacks before damage occurs.  

---

## Quick Recap (Mnemonic)  
- **Detect vs Prevent**  
  - **IDS → Detect Only**  
  - **IPS → Detect + Stop**
