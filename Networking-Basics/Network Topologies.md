## What are Network Topologies?  
- **Definition:** The physical or logical layout of how devices are connected in a network.  
- **Purpose:** Determines how data flows, how devices communicate, and how resilient the network is.  
- **Analogy:** Like different road layouts in a city affecting how traffic moves.  

---

## The 5 Common Network Topologies  

### 1. Bus Topology (Step 1)  
- **Function:** All devices share a single communication line.  
- **Role:** Data is broadcast to all devices.  
- **Examples:** Old Ethernet networks.  

---

### 2. Star Topology (Step 2)  
- **Function:** All devices connect to a central hub/switch.  
- **Role:** Central device controls communication.  
- **Examples:** Modern LAN networks.  

---

### 3. Ring Topology (Step 3)  
- **Function:** Devices connected in a circular loop.  
- **Role:** Data travels in one direction around the ring.  
- **Examples:** Token Ring networks.  

---

### 4. Mesh Topology (Step 4)  
- **Function:** Each device connects to multiple other devices.  
- **Role:** Provides redundancy and multiple paths.  
- **Examples:** Internet backbone networks.  

---

### 5. Hybrid Topology (Step 5)  
- **Function:** Combination of two or more topologies.  
- **Role:** Flexible and scalable network design.  
- **Examples:** Enterprise networks combining star + mesh.  

---

## Why It Matters  
- **Performance:** Affects speed and efficiency.  
- **Scalability:** Determines how easily network grows.  
- **Fault Tolerance:** Impacts network reliability.  

---

## Quick Recap (Mnemonic)  
- **Bus → Star → Ring → Mesh → Hybrid**  
  - **Line → Center → Loop → Many → Mix**
