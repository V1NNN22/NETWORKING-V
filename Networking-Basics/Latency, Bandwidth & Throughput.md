## What are Latency, Bandwidth & Throughput?  
- **Definition:** Core performance metrics used to measure how efficiently data travels across a network.  
- **Purpose:** Helps analyze speed, delay, and actual data transfer efficiency in networking.  
- **Analogy:** Like a highway system where latency is travel time, bandwidth is road width, and throughput is actual cars reaching destination.  

---

## The 3 Core Concepts  

### 1. Latency  
- **Function:** Time taken for a packet to travel from source to destination.  
- **Role:** Measures delay in communication.  
- **Examples:** Ping time in milliseconds (ms).  

---

### 2. Bandwidth  
- **Function:** Maximum data capacity of a network link.  
- **Role:** Defines how much data can be transmitted per second.  
- **Examples:** 100 Mbps, 1 Gbps connections.  

---

### 3. Throughput  
- **Function:** Actual data successfully transferred over the network.  
- **Role:** Real-world performance measurement.  
- **Examples:** Effective speed after overhead and losses.  

---

## Key Differences  

| Metric | Meaning | Unit | Focus |
|--------|--------|------|-------|
| Latency | Delay | ms | Speed of response |
| Bandwidth | Capacity | Mbps/Gbps | Maximum limit |
| Throughput | Actual transfer | Mbps/Gbps | Real performance |

---

## Why It Matters  
- **Performance Optimization:** Helps identify bottlenecks.  
- **Network Design:** Ensures proper capacity planning.  
- **User Experience:** Affects streaming, gaming, and browsing quality.  

---

## Quick Recap (Mnemonic)  
- **Delay → Capacity → Actual**  
  - **Latency → Bandwidth → Throughput**
