## What is a Firewall?  
- **Definition:** A security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predefined rules.  
- **Purpose:** Protects networks from unauthorized access and malicious traffic → acts as a barrier between trusted and untrusted networks.  
- **Analogy:** Like a security guard checking who can enter or leave a building.  

---

## The 4 Core Steps of Firewall Operation  

### 1. Traffic Monitoring (Step 1)  
- **Function:** Firewall inspects incoming and outgoing packets.  
- **Role:** Captures packet details like IP, port, protocol.  
- **Examples:** Monitoring HTTP, FTP, SSH traffic.  

---

### 2. Rule Matching (Step 2)  
- **Function:** Packet is compared against firewall rules.  
- **Role:** Determines whether traffic is allowed or blocked.  
- **Examples:** Allow port 80, block port 23 (Telnet).  

---

### 3. Decision Making (Step 3)  
- **Function:** Firewall decides action based on rules.  
- **Role:** Allow, deny, or log the traffic.  
- **Examples:** Dropping suspicious packets.  

---

### 4. Logging & Alerts (Step 4)  
- **Function:** Records traffic activity and triggers alerts.  
- **Role:** Helps in monitoring and incident response.  
- **Examples:** Logging intrusion attempts.  

---

## Types of Firewalls  
- **Packet Filtering Firewall:** Filters packets based on headers.  
- **Stateful Firewall:** Tracks active connections.  
- **Application Layer Firewall:** Inspects application-level data.  
- **Next-Gen Firewall (NGFW):** Includes deep packet inspection and intrusion prevention.  

---

## Why It Matters  
- **Security:** First line of defense against cyber attacks.  
- **Traffic Control:** Regulates network communication.  
- **Monitoring:** Provides visibility into network activity.  

---

## Quick Recap (Mnemonic)  
- **Inspect → Match → Decide → Log**  
  - **Traffic → Rules → Action → Record**
